# Portal System

## Purpose

Portals are the core chaos engine of the game.

They create danger, optional risk, strange trades, extreme boss situations, and build-defining rewards.

## Portal philosophy

Portals should make the player think:

> This is probably a bad idea... but my build might actually abuse it.

## Portal outcome categories

### Boss chaos

Examples:

- Spawn two bosses.
- Spawn an early endboss.
- Spawn a boss with extra modifiers.
- Spawn a boss while the normal wave continues.

### Cursed trade NPC

An NPC appears and offers one positive and one negative effect.

Example:

- Positive: +50% attack speed.
- Negative: -30% direct weapon damage.

This is bad for normal builds, but strong for poison, burn, summons, on-hit, or effect-based builds.

### Swarm event

More enemies spawn for a limited time.

This is dangerous, but useful for builds that scale from kills, XP, coins, lifesteal, explosions, or on-death effects.

### Mutation event

The portal changes how a build works.

Example:

- Critical hits no longer deal bonus damage, but every crit creates a small explosion.

### Greed event

The portal offers major rewards with major danger.

Example:

- Rewards doubled, but enemies gain speed and damage until the event ends.

## Portal stats

### Portal Luck

Increases the chance for rare or beneficial portal outcomes.

### Portal Frequency

Increases how often portals appear.

### Portal Instability

Increases the chance for extreme outcomes. Higher danger, higher reward.

### Portal Reward Multiplier

Increases the value of rewards earned from portal events.

### Corruption

Represents accumulated dangerous reality damage. Some builds want corruption.

## Design rule

Every portal negative should have at least one build path that can exploit it.
