# Build Philosophy

## Core rule

Every drawback should have at least one build that can turn it into an advantage.

The first goal is not perfect balance. The first goal is interesting interaction.

## Desired player reaction

Players should experience moments like:

> This build looks terrible. Why is it working?

## Build archetypes

### Curse Hoarder

Wants curses and corruption because they increase power.

### Portal Addict

Wants more portals, stronger portals, and better portal rewards.

### Last Stand

Stays at low HP and converts danger into damage, speed, or defense.

### Swarm Farmer

Wants more enemies because kills create resources, healing, explosions, or scaling.

### Boss Hunter

Wants bosses to spawn more often or in multiples because boss kills grant long-term rewards.

### Status Engine

Uses poison, burn, bleed, fear, frost, or other effects instead of relying on direct weapon damage.

### On-Hit Machine

Uses attack speed and many small hits to trigger effects repeatedly.

### Sacrifice Build

Gives up HP, armor, speed, weapons, or items to gain unusual scaling.

## Item design rules

Avoid flat stat-only items when possible.

Simple item:

- +5% damage.

Better item:

- Gain +4% damage for each active curse.

Stronger design:

- Gain +4% damage for each active curse. At 5 curses, portals spawn 20% more often.

## Balance rule

The game may allow overpowered runs sometimes.

Overpowered runs are acceptable when they feel earned through risk, synergy, or smart exploitation.
